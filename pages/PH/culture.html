The Philippine Culture


Filipino culture is very distinct and considered rich and diversified. According to Dumaraos (2018), there are a few things about Philippine culture that make it different from the rest of the world. She listed these things and will be discussed in this article.


First, Filipinos are well-known for their huge spirit of resiliency. They always manage to rise above different challenges such as calamities, catastrophes and instead of wallowing, they manage to pick themselves up and put a big happy smile on their faces. Filipinos take pride in their families. In the Philippines, the people are very family-oriented, and they prioritize their family first. Sometimes, even the closest of friends are considered family too.


Filipinos are very religious as well. In all corners of a Filipino house, you can find brazen images of crosses and other religious paraphernalia. They go to church every Sunday, or sometimes even twice or three times a week.


Filipino people are very respectful as well as from the moment they are born into this world, they are taught already how to be respectful by using simple catchphrases like “po” and “opo” especially when addressing elders. They have a culture of “pagmamano” or raising the backs of the hand of their elders to their foreheads, again as a sign of utmost respect.


They also have this culture of “bayanihan” or “community spirit” where Filipinos help one another without expecting anything in return. Filipinos are very helpful and selfless. The Filipino people also value traditions and culture. For them, traditions at the home and in their family are important.


They usually set aside a specific day for a certain celebration like festivals, birthday parties, reunions, etc. And of course, every gathering is dedicated to keeping up with each other over sumptuous food. On the other hand, in the Philippines, they love to hold different celebrations or fiestas. Every part of the country has its own kind of festival especially to honor their patron saint or to celebrate abundance in agriculture and such. For example, Bacolod has its Maskara Festival, Davao has its Kadayawan Festival and Marinduque has its own Moriones Festival. These are just a few examples.


Filipinos also have the longest Christmas celebrations ever. Even as early as August, you can hear Christmas songs and jingles being played in the malls or in the restaurants in the Philippines. The mood becomes festive, with many people shopping and in good spirits. Christmas celebrations last until around the first or second week of January.


Filipinos are very fond of eating. Aside from breakfast, lunch, and dinner, Filipinos manage to squeeze in a little meal in between, too. Whether they eat every hour or every three hours, they savor every bite. They also love to sing, and therefore karaoke has become so prevalent and favored in the country. As part of their recreation, Filipinos spend time with their families and friends singing or belting out new and old songs.


Filipinos also love art and architecture. Just look at the massive and tall buildings everywhere. Filipinos have a penchant for bringing art and architecture to a whole new level. They love to design creatively, think intuitively and have a passion for anything different and unique.
