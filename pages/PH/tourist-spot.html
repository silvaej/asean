     Tubbataha Reef in Sulu Sea is a marine sanctuary, it is made up of two atolls, North Atoll and South Atoll, separated by a deep channel of approximately 5 miles (8 km) wide. Tubbataha Reef is one of the most popular dive sites in the Philippines because of its coral walls where the shallow coral reef abruptly ends giving way to great depths.





     Mayon Volcano is famous for its absolutely symmetrical cone shape, which rises over 8,000 feet (2,400 meters) above sea level. Camping, climbing, hiking, bird viewing, and photography are just some of the activities available to visitors. Mayon has erupted 49 times in the last 400 years, making it the Philippines' most active volcano. Mayon's most devastating eruption occurred on February 1, 1814, when volcanic rocks rained down on the adjacent communities. The belfry and the remains of the baroque church that was destroyed by the 1814 eruption are still visible.





     The attractive coastal resort of Puerto Galera, located just south of Manila, is known for its beautiful beaches, good diving spots, and diverse marine life. White Beach and Sabang Beach are the most popular beaches, with nearby shopping, dining, nightlife, and hotel accommodations. Muelle Bay's natural harbor, with its rows of stores, restaurants, and bars, is another popular sight. Visitors can also go snorkeling or diving among old shipwrecks and hundreds of fish species to witness some of Asia's best coral reefs.





     Puerto Princesa, on the northern coast of the island of Palawa, is a naturalist's dream. This wonderful town is home to unspoiled landscapes rich with nature, as well as one of the world's most unusual natural occurrences, the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River. This natural wonder, which is protected within a national park, is the world's longest navigable underground river. Paddle boat tours with a guide highlight fascinating rock formations and fluttering bats.





     The Chocolate Hills are an unique geological structure that consists of at least 1,268 individual mounds spread throughout the interior of the island of Bohol. They are one of the top tourist attractions in the Philippines. The nearly symmetrical and similar-sized formations are covered in green grass and range in height from 98 to 164 feet (30 to 50 meters). The grass turns brow during the dry season, hence the name. There is no understanding on how these massive mole hills were formed. The Chocolate Hills are thought to be weathered rock formations of a type of coastal limestone on top of an impervious layer of clay, according to one idea.





     Boracay is a small island with a lot to offer, including award-winning beaches, lovely resorts, and exciting activities such as cliff diving, parasailing, motorbiking, horseback riding, snorkeling, kite surfing, and scuba diving. If that isn't enough, guests can take boat trips to see breathtaking sunsets, explore volcanic caverns, and visit turquoise lagoon coves. Boracay's nightlife pulsates after the sun sets, with several clubs and restaurants serving food, beverages, and entertainment till daybreak
